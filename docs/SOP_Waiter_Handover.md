# Waiter Handover Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

**Document Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** 2026-04-26  
**Department:** Food & Beverage - Waiter Service  
**Applicable To:** All Waitstaff, Bartenders, Shift Supervisors

---

## 1. PURPOSE & SCOPE

### Purpose
To ensure seamless shift transitions, maintain absolute inventory accuracy, and enforce financial accountability during waiter handovers. This SOP standardizes the process of reconciling stock levels, recording sales usage, documenting breakage/wastage, and balancing cash drawers between consecutive shifts.

### Scope
This procedure applies to all waiting staff, bartenders, and service personnel operating in the liquor club premises during operational hours. It covers:
- Opening stock calculation and verification
- Real-time usage recording throughout the shift
- Closing physical stock counts
- Breakage and wastage documentation
- Cash drawer reconciliation
- Formal handover communication between outgoing and incoming staff

## 2. SHIFT TIMINGS & HANDOVER SCHEDULE

### Standard Shifts
| Shift | Handover Time | Duration |
|-------|---------------|----------|
| Morning | 09:00 - 17:00 | 8 hours |
| Evening | 17:00 - 01:00 | 8 hours |
| Night | 01:00 - 09:00 | 8 hours |

### Handover Window
- **Grace Period:** 15 minutes before and after scheduled shift change
- **Mandatory Presence:** Both outgoing and incoming staff must be present for the full handover process
- **Supervisor Oversight:** Shift Supervisor must countersign all handovers during audit periods

---

## 3. PRE-HANDOVER PREPARATION (15 Minutes Prior)

### Outgoing Staff Responsibilities
1. **Cease new orders** 10 minutes before handover time
2. **Complete all pending orders** and ensure customer satisfaction
3. **Begin preliminary stock assessment** in service areas
4. **Prepare cash drawer** for counting (remove large bills, organize by denomination)
5. **Gather required documentation:**
   - Current shift's sales receipts
   - Breakage/wastage log
   - Previous shift's handover checklist
   - Stock count sheets

### Incoming Staff Responsibilities
1. **Arrive 5 minutes early** to observe current operations
2. **Review previous shift's handover report** for pending issues
3. **Inspect service station setup** and equipment condition

## 4. HANDOVER PROCEDURE STEP-BY-STEP

### Phase 1: Opening Stock Verification (10 minutes)

#### 4.1 Opening Stock Calculation Formula
```
Opening Stock = Previous Closing Stock
               + Restock Received During Previous Shift
               - Sales Usage (from previous shift)
               + Returns/Exchanges
               - Breakage/Wastage (documented)
```

#### 4.2 Verification Process
1. **Retrieve previous shift's closing stock count** from handover sheet
2. **Cross-reference with inventory management system** (if available)
3. **Physically verify opening stock** in designated service areas:
   - Bar counters (bottles, kegs, display units)
   - Storage rooms (shelves, coolers, dry storage)
   - Wine cellar (if applicable)
4. **Document discrepancies immediately** on the Handover Discrepancy Form (Appendix A)
5. **Sign and date** the Opening Stock Verification Sheet (Form F&B-HO-01)

#### 4.3 Acceptance Criteria
- Physical count must match documented closing stock within ±2% variance
- Any variance >2% requires immediate supervisor notification
- Unresolved discrepancies must be documented and signed by both parties

---

### Phase 2: Real-Time Usage Recording (Throughout Shift)

#### 5.1 Sales Usage Documentation
**Method:** Update stock consumption log in real-time for each order

| Time | Item | Quantity | Unit | Order # | Staff Initials |
|------|------|----------|------|---------|----------------|
| 14:30 | Jack Daniel's | 1 | bottle | ORD-001 | JD |
| 15:45 | Grey Goose Vodka | 0.5 | bottle | ORD-015 | MK |

**Procedure:**
1. Record every dispensed item immediately after order completion
2. Include precise measurements (bottles, shots, gallons, kegs)
3. Note order reference number for audit trail
4. Initial each entry for accountability

#### 5.2 Breakage/Wastage Logging

**Breakage Categories:**
- **Spillage:** Accidental liquid loss during service
- **Spoilage:** Expired or contaminated product
- **Theft:** Unauthorized consumption (must be reported to supervisor immediately)
- **Sample/Demo:** Complimentary tastings (requires manager approval)
- **Equipment Malfunction:** Loss due to faulty equipment

**Breakage/Wastage Form (Form F&B-BW-02):**

| Time | Item | Quantity | Reason | Value (KES) | Witness | Staff Initials |
|------|------|----------|--------|-------------|---------|----------------|
| 16:20 | Moet & Chandon | 1 bottle | Spillage during pour | 12,000 | PT | JD |
| 18:45 | Heineken Draft | 2 liters | Faulty tap system | 800 | MK | PT |

**Requirements:**
- All breakage must be witnessed and co-signed by at least one other staff member
- Theft must be reported immediately to Shift Supervisor
- High-value items (>KES 5,000) require manager authorization

---

### Phase 3: Closing Stock Count (30 Minutes Before Shift End)

#### 6.1 Physical Inventory Count Procedure

**Count Areas & Priority:**
1. **High-Value Items** (Champagne, Premium Spirits) - Count first
2. **Medium-Value Items** (Standard Spirits, Wine) - Count second
3. **Low-Value Items** (Beer, Mixers, Shots) - Count third

**Counting Method:**
- Use calibrated measuring tools for partial bottles/kegs
- Record to nearest 0.1 unit (e.g., 0.7 bottle remaining)
- For kegs: weigh and convert to volume using standard conversion charts
- Perform **double-count verification**: count once, then recount by different staff member

**Closing Stock Count Sheet (Form F&B-CS-03):**

| Item | Opening Stock | Sales Usage | Breakage | Closing Count | Variance | Notes |
|------|---------------|-------------|----------|---------------|----------|-------|
| Jack Daniel's | 24 bottles | 3 bottles | 0 | 21 bottles | 0 | ✓ |
| Grey Goose | 12 bottles | 2.5 bottles | 0.5 bottle spill | 9 bottles | 0 | ✓ |

#### 6.2 Variance Investigation
- **Acceptable Variance:** 0 to +0.2 units (rounding tolerance)
- **Investigate if:** Negative variance >0.1 units OR positive variance >0.5 units
- Document all variances on the Discrepancy Report (Form F&B-DR-04)

#### 7.2 Sales Revenue Calculation

```
Total Sales = Σ (All Sales Receipts for Shift)
Cash Expected = (Total Sales - Card Payments - Mobile Payments)
```

#### 7.3 Cash Balance Verification

```
Expected Cash on Hand = Starting Float + Cash Sales - Cash Expenses (if any)
Actual Cash Count = Physical cash in drawer at shift end
 Variance = Expected Cash – Actual Cash Count
```

**Variance Thresholds:**
- **KES 0 - 500:** Acceptable (rounding, minor errors)
- **KES 501 - 2,000:** Document and investigate
- **KES > 2,000:** Escalate to Shift Supervisor immediately

### Phase 5: Formal Handover Briefing (10 Minutes)

#### 8.1 Structured Communication Protocol

**Outgoing Staff Must Report:**

1. **Inventory Status:**
   - Items running low (<20% of opening stock)
   - Items expired/near expiry
   - Damaged goods requiring replacement

2. **Customer Information:**
   - Large parties on tab/credit
   - VIP guests with special requests
   - Outstanding complaints or issues

3. **Equipment & Facilities:**
   - Faulty equipment (taps, coolers, POS)
   - Maintenance issues (plumbing, lighting)
   - Supply shortages (napkins, glassware, etc.)

4. **Financial Notes:**
   - Unexplained cash variance
   - Refunds issued
   - Complimentary items given (manager-approved)

## 5. POST-HANDOVER RESPONSIBILITIES

### 9.1 Incoming Staff Actions (First 30 Minutes)
1. **Verify opening stock** matches documented figures
2. **Set up service stations** according to standard layout
3. **Test equipment** (POS, taps, coolers)
4. **Review reservation book** and special requests
5. **Check cash drawer** float amount

### 9.2 Outgoing Staff Actions (First 30 Minutes)
1. **Remain available** for 15 minutes post-handover for questions
2. **Secure personal belongings** and clock out
3. **Submit completed documentation** to supervisor's office
4. **Report any urgent issues** via shift logbook

## 7. QUALITY CONTROL & AUDIT

### 7.1 Daily Supervisor Checks
- Randomly verify 10% of handover checks
- Review all breakage logs for patterns
- Audit cash variances >KES 500
- Conduct weekly training refreshers for staff with errors

### 7.2 Monthly Management Review
- Analyze inventory shrinkage trends
- Review high-value breakage incidents
- Evaluate cash drawer variance patterns
- Update SOP based on operational feedback

### 7.3quarterly Internal Audit
- Full physical inventory count comparison
- Documentation compliance check
- Staff competency assessment
- Process improvement recommendations

---

## 8. NON-COMPLIANCE & DISCIPLINARY ACTION

### Violations Subject to Disciplinary Action
1. **Failure to complete handover documentation** – Written warning
2. **Falsifying records or counts** – Immediate suspension, investigation
3. **Unauthorized consumption of inventory** – Termination
4. **Repeated cash variances (>KES 2,000 twice in 30 days)** – Demotion or termination
5. **Leaving shift without proper handover** – Loss of shift privileges
